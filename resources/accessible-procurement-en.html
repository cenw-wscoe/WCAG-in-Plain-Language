Accessible Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Procurement

As a result of the Accessible Canada Act royal ascension on June 21, 2019, Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) has to provide barrier-free services by 2025. (ESDC) has created an Accessibility Roadmap, which includes the vision of making ICT usable by all.

Procurement, development, and use of (ICT) hardware and software in (ESDC) do not consistently reflect accessibility requirements. Although the Department’s public-facing solutions are already compliant with accessibility standards, an inventory has determined that almost none of the internal solutions are meeting accessibility standards. As of December 2020, less than half of the new ICT procurement are 100% compliant to the Treasury Board Secretariat’s (TBS) accessibility standards.

In order to assist (ESDC)’s managers to procure accessible ICTs, the IT Accessibility Office has developed accessible procurement guidelines, accessibility requirements checklists for ICT products and services based on Standard EN 301 549 and Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs).
