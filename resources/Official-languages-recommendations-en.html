Official Languages (OL) Recommendations

Unilingual meeting or event (recommended)

  • If possible, plan two sessions: one in English and one in French in order to accommodate all employees (regardless of disability or not).
  • It avoids excluding unilingual participants and simplifies the use of interpretation and captioning services. It is also specifically beneficial for employees with cognitive disabilities.
  • Note: the recommendation on bilingual meetings for the Commissionaire of official languages is to alternate the session language, for example one showcase in English and the next in French. In the meeting invite, offer the possibility (if required or requested) to have the session in the other language.
  • For more information on simultaneous interpretation and closed captioning, you can consult the document Workaround for Interpretations in Teams provided by the Engagement Communication team who organize the Watercooler event at ESDC. The cost is approximately $1000 per unilingual event. They mention that comparing the service with the cost and accessibility value, it was more cost effective to organize unilingual events than bilingual.

Bilingual meeting or event (not recommended)

  • Avoid switching between languages frequently.
  • Add a summary in the other official language after each topic or logical unit, clearly identifying a summary of what was said. This has the advantage to also be a learning opportunity from an OL standpoint and does not add a lot of time.
