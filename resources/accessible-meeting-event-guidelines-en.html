Accessible Meeting/Event Guidelines

Prior to meeting or event

  • For a small-scale meeting, get to know the participants’ needs and ask the small attendance group if they require any additional accommodations for the small meeting.
  • For a large-scale event, send event invitation 3 weeks prior and ask attendees if they require a specific accommodation (for example a sign language interpreter or CART).
  • For an open-door type meeting where you have no way of knowing the needs of your participants, then you should provide as many accommodations as possible, including but not limited to CART (Communication Access Real-Time), sign language interpreters in both American Sign Language (ASL - English) and Langue des signes Québécoise (LSQ - French).
    • Please see CART services and Sign Language Interpreters (SLI) for additional information regarding CART services and SLI.
  • Ensure all meeting-related emails, documents, and presentations are accessible Accessible Documents - ESDC / IT Accessibility office.
  • Send both unilingual (French and English) documents/presentations one week prior to the meeting. The presentations should also include the translated speaker notes.
    • Please see Official Languages (OL) recommendations for additional information regarding Official Languages recommendations.
  • In the meeting invite always indicate that employees who require accommodations (captions, CART, large fonts, etc.) should contact the organizer as soon as possible in order to ensure availability of services.
  • Ensure that presenters have consulted the Planning Inclusive and Accessible Events Internal link document, the section on tips for presenters.
  • Provide multiple ways to join the meeting such as dial-in.
  • Do a “Dry Run” a couple of days before the event to make sure everything runs smoothly.

Remind presenters to:

  • Use plain language and explain all terms and acronyms.
  • Announce their name before speaking.
  • Ensure a plain and non-distracting background.
  • Ensure adequate lighting when sharing video for individuals who lip read.

During the meeting or event

  • When possible and with the consent of all participants, set the meeting to be recorded in order to allow participants to review the presentation and take notes at their own pace.
  • Mute participants upon entry and inform them of this.
  • Introduce interpreters and/or captioners.
  • Send link to captioning service in Chat and by email.
  • Remind participants how to “pin” interpreters and speakers if needed.
  • Remind participants of in-event etiquette:
    • Log out of VPN
    • Stay on mute when not speaking
    • Keep camera off if not presenting, to reduce visual stimuli except the presenter
    • If a participant feels comfortable, they can turn their camera on when speaking for participants who lip read
    • Indicate who is speaking every time the speaker changes
    • Speak at a moderate pace and enunciate clearly
    • Describe all visual material (such as PowerPoints and screen shares)
      • If using MS Teams, encourage presenters to use the Share PowerPoint feature instead of just sharing their screen to allow assistive technology users to engage with the PowerPoint.
    • Read relevant Chat messages aloud and you can also mention to participants to only use the chat for questions.
    • Use the Raise your hand feature to ask questions
    • Send relevant links shared in Chat by email
